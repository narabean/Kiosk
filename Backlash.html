Religious Controversy: The Beatles faced strong opposition from conservative figures, including William F. Buckley and religious leaders who decried their impact on youth.

Boycott and Record Burnings: Southern radio stations stopped playing Beatles records, religious groups were burning albums.

Drug Use: Apparent to the audience by 1967 because of a McCartney interview, references to LSD, acid, and cannabis.

In 1966, John Lennon's assertion that the Beatles were more popular than Jesus sparked widespread controversy in the U.S., leading to protests, record boycotts, and public burnings. Though Lennon later clarified his statement, the incident underscored the intersection of music, celebrity, and cultural sensitivities during the turbulent 1960s.
